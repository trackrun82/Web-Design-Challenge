Humidity

Each dot on this plot represents one of the over 500 random cities in the study. This plot is showing that humidity tends to increase as a city is further away from the equator. This trend seemed consistent in both the Northern and Southern hemispheres and is investigated further below:

The humidity of a city seems to slightly increase with proximity to the equator as seen in both linear regressions trending towards 0 latitude. Both hemispheres predicted approximately the same humidity near the equator and also a similar trend as a city's proximity increased from the equator. However, both hemispheres showed very varied data and very low r-squared values indicating humidity cannot reliably be predicted solely by the latitude of a city.
